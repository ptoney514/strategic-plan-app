# StrataDASH Product Review

**Date:** April 5, 2026  
**Purpose:** Assess current product position, stage, risks, and next milestones.

## Product Summary

**Product name:** StrataDASH  
**Purpose:** StrataDASH helps school districts publish strategic-plan progress in a public, read-only format so parents, taxpayers, board members, and community stakeholders can quickly understand district goals, progress, and outcomes.

**Site goal:** Convert district leaders, superintendents, communications teams, and strategy/admin leaders into demo requests or trial signups by showing that StrataDASH makes strategic reporting clear, credible, and easy to share. The product should be positioned as a simpler, more public-friendly alternative to broader strategic planning tools like OnStrategy.

## Users and Jobs

**Primary buyers:** District administrators, superintendents, cabinet leaders, communications directors, and strategy or assessment teams that need a clean public-facing way to report progress.

**Secondary buyers:** Board members and nonprofit or public-sector leaders with similar transparency and reporting needs.

**Public end users:** Parents, taxpayers, and stakeholders who want to check district progress without training, a login, or insider knowledge.

**Core job to be done:** Help districts explain what they are trying to do, how progress is tracking, and what results are happening in a format the public can understand quickly.

## Problem and Differentiation

School strategic plans are often buried in PDFs, hard to interpret, and difficult to keep updated in a way the community can actually follow. Existing tools in this category tend to emphasize internal planning workflows, action plans, execution management, and broad strategic software capabilities. StrataDASH's advantage is a clearer public reporting experience built around transparency, readability, and trust.

**Primary value proposition:** Give your community a clear view of what the district is trying to do, how it is progressing, and what results are happening.

**Core differentiators:**

- Public-first reporting instead of internal-first strategy management
- Read-only stakeholder experience with no login or training required
- Education-specific positioning rather than generic enterprise strategy software
- Faster understanding through cleaner presentation of goals, progress, and outcomes

## Current Process Status

**Current stage:** Pre-pilot MVP / early validation

**Why this is the right stage label:** The product foundation appears materially built, including public dashboard views, authentication, Excel import, metric builder capability, and a functioning admin system. However, commercial and workflow validation is still limited. There is currently one known user expected to be ready to use the product around June 2026, and that first real implementation is likely to generate the most important learning so far.

**What is built now:**

- Public dashboard views for district strategic-plan reporting
- Admin and authentication infrastructure
- Excel import workflow
- Metric builder and progress display foundation
- Production deployment and database-backed application architecture

**What is still being proven:**

- How easily a real district can set up and maintain the dashboard
- Whether district staff can keep progress updates current without heavy support
- Whether public users understand the reporting quickly and trust the presentation
- Whether the product reduces friction compared with existing reporting methods
- Whether the buyer value proposition is strong enough to support repeat sales

## Strengths

- The product solves a real communication problem that districts already have.
- The audience definition is strong and separates buyers from public viewers clearly.
- The positioning is distinct enough to avoid direct feature-for-feature competition with broader strategy platforms.
- The product promise, "fast understanding without friction," is simple and defensible.
- The application appears past pure concept stage and into usable MVP territory.

## Gaps and Risks

- Only one near-term user means market learning is still thin.
- There is not yet clear proof of repeatable onboarding, adoption, or renewal behavior.
- Admin usability and reporting workflows may still need refinement before the product scales.
- Trust signals are not yet proven through case studies, district references, or measured public engagement.
- Pricing, packaging, and implementation effort are not yet clearly validated in the market.

## Next Milestones

1. Get the first district implementation live and document the full onboarding process.
2. Observe where setup, updates, publishing, and stakeholder comprehension break down.
3. Capture evidence from that rollout, including screenshots, quotes, outcomes, and workflow friction.
4. Refine the admin experience and messaging based on what the first district actually struggles with.
5. Convert the first implementation into a proof point that supports broader sales and marketing.

## Exit Criteria for the Current Stage

StrataDASH should move from pre-pilot MVP into validated pilot stage when the following are true:

- One district is actively using the product in a real reporting cycle
- Core admin workflows can be completed with limited founder intervention
- Public stakeholders can understand goals and progress quickly
- The team has concrete feedback on onboarding effort, update cadence, and reporting clarity
- The product has at least one credible proof point for future sales conversations

## Bottom Line

StrataDASH is not at idea stage. It appears to be an operational MVP with strong early positioning, but it is still before real market validation. The period between now and the first expected district rollout around June 2026 matters because that usage should clarify whether the current product is merely functional or truly repeatable, credible, and ready to scale.
